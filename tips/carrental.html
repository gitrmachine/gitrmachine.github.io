Car Rentals

If Google gives you a driving route that’s going to take an hour, plan on 2 hours! You’ll find that the scenery slows you down, as well as the slower speed limits and driving through towns and villages. This is all part of the extraordinary charm of Ireland.

One of the terms you’ll encounter as you research car rental options is collision damage waiver (CDW). This can double the amount of your rental, so we advise that you check with your credit card to see if they cover CDW in Ireland. In the event that they do, you can waive this coverage during your rental period. When checking on this with your credit card company, be very specific about asking if they cover CDW in Ireland, as it is sometimes specifically excluded. Perhaps you already know that traffic in Ireland drives on the left. Also, speed limits are posted in kilometers/hour. Combine this with jet-lagged travelers arriving on early morning flights, and driving can quickly become a dangerous activity. The Road Safety Authority in Ireland published this helpful guide particularly geared towards tourists.
